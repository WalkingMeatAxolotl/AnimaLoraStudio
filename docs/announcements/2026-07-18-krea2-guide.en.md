Starting with 0.20.0 you can train LoRAs for **Krea 2** (a 12.9B single-stream MMDiT) with the same pipeline — scraping, tagging, training, and image-gen testing all work, and the output LoRA drops straight into ComfyUI. This is the quick guide plus basic training tips; the full version lives in [Training tips → Krea 2 training](https://github.com/WalkingMeatAxolotl/AnimaLoraStudio/blob/master/docs/user-guide/training-tips.md#krea-2-训练).

### Getting started

1. Go to the model download center under **Settings → Training** and grab the weights in the "Krea 2" section (governed by the Krea 2 Community License):
   - **Raw**: the training base model. bf16 (26.3 GB, for 32 GB GPUs) or **official fp8** (13.1 GB — the pick for 24 GB-class GPUs)
   - **Turbo**: optional distilled model for fast test generation (8 steps); also available in fp8
   - **Text encoder** (Qwen3-VL): bf16 (8.9 GB) or fp8 (5.2 GB), switchable via radio
   - The VAE is shared with Anima — no second download needed
2. On the training config page, switch **Model family** to Krea 2 — a confirmation dialog lists every path and default about to be recomputed; confirm and you're set. Anima and Krea 2 versions can coexist in one project.

### Training tips

- **Train on Raw, never on Turbo.** Turbo is a distilled inference model; even if Turbo is selected globally, new training versions automatically fall back to Raw. A LoRA trained on Raw loads directly on Turbo for generation.
- **VRAM**: on a 24 GB GPU, train with the official fp8 base (just pick the fp8 file — no extra switch), and leave some peak headroom at 1024²; 32 GB runs both, with bf16 cutting it close. fp8 training requires `grad_checkpoint` (on by default; turning it off errors at startup); DoRA is incompatible with fp8 bases, while LoRA / LoHa / LoKr / rs-LoRA all work.
- **Caption in natural language**: Krea 2 uses the Qwen3-VL text encoder, not the booru tag ecosystem. Use the **LLM tagger** for long descriptive captions; trigger words are injected as usual. Tag shuffle / keep tokens / tag dropout don't apply to Krea 2 and are hidden automatically. Captions are truncated to 512 tokens during training.
- **The defaults are the right starting point**: timestep sampling defaults to `krea2_shift` (resolution-aware dynamic shift, matching the official training recipe); sampler Euler + Simple, 28 steps, CFG 4.5; the text cache is on by default — all captions are pre-encoded at training start and the text encoder is then released, saving about 9 GB of VRAM.
- Learning rate, rank (default 32/32), optimizers and the rest follow the same guidance as Anima — see the training tips doc.

### Generation & ComfyUI

- Switch the model family to Krea 2 on the Test page to generate; selecting Turbo automatically applies 8 steps / no CFG. fp8 bases roughly halve weight VRAM — a 16 GB GPU can generate with **Settings → Testing → VRAM policy** set to "save VRAM".
- The **Euler + Simple** shown here corresponds word-for-word to **euler + simple** when using Krea 2 in ComfyUI — no translation needed to reproduce results.
- Community Krea 2 LoRAs from civitai (PEFT / comfy key format) load directly on the Test page.

### If you only use Anima

Nothing to do — the default family is still Anima, and existing projects, presets, and workflows are unchanged.
